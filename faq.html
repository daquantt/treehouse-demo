Crystallization is a natural process and not a sign of adulteration or spoilage. To get rid of unwanted crystals, submerge the bottle in warm water until they return to liquid form.

The colour and taste of honey are influenced by the type of flowers from which bees gather nectar. Each geographical location produces its own unique, distinct properties. The consistency of honey on the other hand is influenced by the seasons we experience in Trinidad and Tobago - in the dry season, the honey tends to be thicker due to a low water content and in the rainy season, honey is usually runny due to a high water content.

The hexagonal prismatic waxy cells contain:

1. Natural honey - Full of vitamins and minerals inclusive of: calcium, iron, magnesium, zinc, Vitamins C, B6 and B12, A, E, and D

2. Pollen - A significant source of vitamins such as vitamins A, B, D and E and acids such as pantothenic, nicotinic and folic, biotin, rutin, and inositol

3. Royal jelly - Rich in nutrients and contain anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties
